The gap PaedScale encodes

Drug clearance does not scale linearly with body size in early life — the organs that eliminate the drug are still maturing. Scaling an adult dose by weight over-doses the young. PaedScale models the distance between the naïve linear line and the true maturation curve.

How the agent reasons

Python does the arithmetic; Claude does the judgment — mapping the drug to its elimination pathway and maturation curve, then writing the justification.

01 · MECHANISM

Map the pathway

Claude identifies elimination route, enzymes, transporters, active metabolites and protein binding — and it is scored on getting them right, including correct-negatives.

02 · RETRIEVE

Live PK, or abstain

A retrieval subagent scans PubMed (E-utilities) + openFDA for cited adult clearance and Vd. No source, no confident number — it grades D instead of inventing one.

03 · SCALE & GRADE

Compute & concord

The deterministic engine scales by allometry × maturation, then the estimate is checked against a real pediatric guideline (0.67×–1.5×) and graded A → D.

Drug coverage

Eight seeded drugs across three maturation curves and four exposure archetypes. Each reuses an already-sourced ontogeny constant — cite-or-abstain holds.

PaedScale is a research prototype. Output is a defensible starting estimate for a qualified clinician — not an autonomous order, and not a validated clinical-decision-support device. Narrow-therapeutic-index drugs require therapeutic drug monitoring.
